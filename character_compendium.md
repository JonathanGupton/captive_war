# Character Compendium -- The Mercy of Gods (James S.A. Corey)

Comprehensive compilation of every character in the novel, with physical descriptions, history, relationships, plot role, and key quotes.

---

## Section I: Major Human Characters

---

### 1. Dafyd Alkhor

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

> "long charcoal jacket over an embroidered shirt and slim-fitting pants"
> -- Chapter 1 (at the Scholar's Common celebration)

Described as the youngest member of the workgroup. No detailed facial or build descriptions beyond being a young man. During captivity, becomes emaciated; at one point wears only a torn nightgown wrapped around his waist.

#### Character History / Background

Research assistant (scholar-associate rank) at Irvian Research Medrey on Tonner Freis's proteome reconciliation project. Nephew of Dorinda Alkhor, a powerful figure in the funding colloquy. His late mother was Dorinda's sister. His father lives in Astincol with Dafyd's stepmother; the father always wanted to be in research but never got the placement, so Dafyd is "kind of living his dream." Dorinda pushed Dafyd toward Tonner's team as a long-term career investment.

As a child, was terrified of spiders and forced himself to handle them. His mother's observation about him: "My little Dafyd just hates anything telling him what to do." He has a lifelong pattern of defiance -- "a childish need to not be controlled, even by himself."

Politically astute, trained by his aunt. Tonner calls him "a schemer" but Else notes she's "never seen him be cruel."

#### Role & Relationships

- **Tonner Freis:** His team leader. Tonner distrusts him early on but relies on him increasingly in captivity.
- **Else Yannin:** Object of his affection from the start; they become lovers in captivity. He is unaware she harbors an alien spy entity until she reveals it in Chapter 30.
- **Dorinda Alkhor:** His aunt and guardian/mentor. He never sees her again after the invasion.
- **Jessyn:** Close teammate and ally throughout.
- **Jellit:** Becomes a key ally; Jellit forgives Dafyd after the betrayal of the conspiracy.
- **The Carryx Librarian:** Develops a unique working relationship as the sole human intermediary.
- **Ekur-Tkalal:** Appointed by the new librarian as the singular point of contact between all humans and the Carryx.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

**Before the Invasion (Chapters 1-6):**
- Detects at the Scholar's Common that someone is making a play against the workgroup.
- Manipulates conversations with Llaren Morse and his aunt to extract intelligence.
- Warns Else, triggering the emergency team meeting.
- Proposes a quiet political strategy rather than going loud.
- Interrogated by security forces after Samar Austad's death.

**The Catastrophe (Chapters 7-12):**
- Nearly confesses his feelings to Else just before the invasion.
- Survives the five-day war and is identified by name by a Sinen alien.
- Poisoned by the Soft Lothark's death-burst during the failed insurrection; nearly dies.
- Reunited with the full workgroup in the Carryx dormitory quarters.

**Captivity & Rising Leadership (Chapters 13-25):**
- Challenges the librarian about what happens if they refuse to work.
- Forces himself to observe the alien traffic in the cathedral to overcome fear.
- Negotiates writing supplies from the librarian.
- Delivers the pivotal speech reframing the Carryx "test" -- argues they're testing self-organization, not just lab work. This shifts leadership from Tonner to him.
- Begins relationship with Else.
- Uses the Night Drinkers' translator box to communicate with other alien species and locate Jellit's group.

**The Betrayal & Aftermath (Chapters 26-36):**
- Interviews alien species in the cathedral, learning about the Carryx empire.
- Witnesses the massacre of the Night Drinkers.
- Learns from Else that she harbors an alien spy fighting the Carryx's greatest enemy.
- Agonizes but ultimately betrays Ostencour's conspiracy to the Carryx librarian.
- Tackles Tonner at the assembly and breaks his arm to save his life when Tonner challenges the Carryx.
- Realizes Carryx physically metamorphose based on social status.
- Appointed sole intermediary by the new librarian Ekur-Tkalal.
- Declares his intention to wage long-term war against the Carryx from within.

#### Notable Quotes

> "I put people at ease."
> -- Chapter 1

> "If there isn't a quieter way, we can always go loud. But once we've gone loud, we can't go quiet."
> -- Chapter 4

> "What if we don't do it?"
> -- Chapter 13 (challenging the librarian)

> "Either the Carryx are stupid or they're testing us for something besides good lab work. And I think it's the worst mistake we could make to assume the Carryx are stupid."
> -- Chapter 19

> "He is young... We are both young. He didn't mean better. He meant no disrespect. He's young!"
> -- Chapter 34 (shouted to save Tonner)

> "I take responsibility for his correction. He is humbled."
> -- Chapter 34

> "I'm going to learn everything about them. I'm going to figure out how to get in their heads. And I'm going to kill them all and burn their fucking towers to the ground. It's my war now."
> -- Chapter 35

### 2. Else Annalise Yannin

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin) -- host to an alien nano-swarm spy entity

#### Physical Description

> "long, aquiline nose, wide mouth, and thin lips"
> -- Chapter 1

> "threads of premature white in her hair... against the youth of her face, made her seem ageless"
> -- Chapter 1

> Auburn/copper-colored hair ("tucked a lock of auburn hair back behind her ear"). One dimple in her left cheek when she smiled and two on her right. Taps out complex rhythms with her feet when thinking, "like she occupied her body by dancing in place while her mind wandered." Wears an emerald-green dress at the Scholar's Common celebration.

During captivity: hair greasy, "the color of tarnished copper, sticking to her scalp and neck." Bags under bloodshot eyes. Cheekbones sharp with a little indentation behind her chin. Lines at the sides of her mouth that weren't there before.

The Swarm can cause dark motes to shift under her skin and must manually correct her eye color to look human.

#### Character History / Background

Gave up her own research team (spiral analysis, which she recognized was a dead end) to join Tonner's project as second-in-command. Previously worked at an aquaculture farm for six months. Has rooms in the basement level of an old ecosphere, though she mostly lives with Tonner. Smokes marijuana/clove cigarettes. Has a pattern of gravitating toward the man with the most status in a group, something she is ashamed of.

Six months before the Carryx attacked Anjiin, an enemy of the Carryx infiltrated her body with "a billion tiny machines" (the Swarm). She knew the invasion was coming but could not warn anyone without compromising the mission.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Tonner Freis:** Former lover and co-lead of the research group. Their relationship fractures in captivity; she refuses to share a room with him.
- **Dafyd Alkhor:** Becomes her lover in captivity. The Swarm feels something "akin to love" for him.
- **The Swarm:** Shares her body and consciousness with the alien spy entity and the ghost of Ameer Kindred.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

- At the Scholar's Common, Dafyd warns her about the threat to the workgroup.
- Discovers the government is censoring the live feed of alien ships.
- Organizes the bolt-hole during the invasion.
- Becomes emotionally close with Dafyd during the prison transit.
- Declines to share quarters with Tonner, asserting independence.
- The Swarm within her catalogues every detail of the Carryx world-city.
- Reveals the Swarm's existence to Dafyd in Chapter 30.
- Convinces Jellit to cooperate with Dafyd's plan to expose the conspiracy.
- Found dead in Jellit's room during the Carryx purge -- no visible wounds, death unlike the violent Rak-hund killings.

#### Notable Quotes

> "Spiral analysis was a dead end. Everyone knew that. I could jump ship or spend my career driving coffin nails."
> -- Chapter 4

> "I've been through an unpleasant and traumatizing set of experiences... And I find the idea of having a space that's just my own very appealing."
> -- Chapter 12

> "I'm not what you think I am, Dafyd."
> -- Chapter 27

> "There is a war. The Carryx are fighting a vast, terrible war."
> -- Chapter 30

> "I am the weapon of their greatest enemy. I could kill many Carryx before they destroyed me, if I chose to."
> -- Chapter 30

> "We don't have very much left. Not much time. Or safety. Or reason to count on tomorrow. I don't want to sacrifice anything good in my life right now."
> -- Chapter 21

### 3. Tonner Freis

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

> "prematurely gray hair that rose like smoke from an overheated brain"
> -- Chapter 1

> "tight smile"; when he scowls, "he looked like an angry child"
> -- Chapter 1

During captivity: thick, dark beard; face behind it looks like someone else due to extreme weight loss. A bruise covering half his neck. Wild gray halo of hair "defying gravity." Bloodshot, angry eyes. Clothes that look slept in. Fingers of his right hand flutter when lost in thought.

#### Character History / Background

Young, brilliant research lead whose proteome reconciliation project was the first single-term project ever to top all three major ranking lists in the same year -- "the most celebrated mind in the world." Has a "strong intuition for the patterns that living systems fell into." Chose housing near the labs but switched to please Else. Identity deeply tied to his work: "This work is what I am."

His "pathological move" is to focus down on something small enough to control. Has difficulty recognizing his own stress directly -- monitors himself externally for signs like irritability. Else noted: "When he's overstressed, Tonner can get petty. It's his pathology."

#### Role & Relationships

- **Else Yannin:** Lover and partner pre-invasion. Deeply hurt when she moves to Dafyd but tries to contain it.
- **Dafyd Alkhor:** Distrusts him early on ("He's a schemer"), but they develop a working dynamic in captivity.
- **The Workgroup:** Commands loyalty but can be imperious and emotionally volatile. His identity is tied to being the leader.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

- Calls the emergency meeting about the workgroup threat; kicks Rickar out.
- Captured separately with Irinna; witnesses seven deaths during transit, including Daiir Ferria's.
- Rejected by Else when he assumes they'll share a room.
- Organizes the research work schedule and assigns pairs in the Carryx lab.
- Breaks down crying to Else about his inability to protect the team after Irinna's death.
- Successfully synthesizes Jessyn's antidepressant medication from the berries.
- Achieves the breakthrough on feeding the not-turtle using functional enantiomers: "I know how to feed the fucking turtle."
- Loses intellectual leadership to Dafyd in Chapter 19 but retains operational control of the lab.
- Nearly gets himself killed at the Carryx assembly by raising his hand (a Carryx challenge gesture). Dafyd tackles him and breaks his arm to save his life.

#### Notable Quotes

> "This is my project."
> -- Chapter 4

> "He's a schemer. Everything he does is for effect. He's always watching people."
> -- Chapter 4 (about Dafyd)

> "Maybe they kill us. Maybe they ship us back home. Maybe something else. I don't care, and you shouldn't either, because we're not going to fail. This is what we do, and we are going to do the hell out of it."
> -- Chapter 13

> "How do I keep them safe?"
> -- Chapter 18 (to Else, breaking down)

> "I know how to feed the fucking turtle."
> -- Chapter 31

> "I just wanted to make sure we got credit."
> -- Chapter 34

### 4. Jessyn Kaul

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

> "Short, round bodied, and severe."
> -- Chapter 2

Gold-brown skin, black hair and eyes. Has a mole on her right cheek (matching her brother Jellit's). In captivity, transformed: "someone thinner and sharper, like someone had put her on a grindstone and turned her into a knife." Eyes have "a darkness they hadn't had on Anjiin." Holds "her shoulders back and her chin up in constant, unconscious aggression."

#### Character History / Background

Scholar on Tonner's team. Suffers from a serious mental health condition involving intrusive thoughts, anxiety, and depression -- describes herself as "born a little broken." First diagnosed in early adolescence. Her brother Jellit was the one who recognized her spiraling and got her help. She hid her condition from the team to protect her career: "Any little thing, and they won't let you be lead researcher."

Grew up attending a Serrantist church where a priest named Nansui helped her. Her father worked long-distance field contracts; her mother took double shifts. She and Jellit had a shorthand: "Not better, just improved."

#### Role & Relationships

- **Jellit:** Her brother and emotional anchor. Their bond is central to both characters.
- **Irinna:** Close friend and lab partner. Irinna's death devastates her.
- **Campar:** Close friend; he calls her "little sister."
- **The Workgroup:** Becomes the de facto "war leader" against the Night Drinkers.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

**Before Captivity:**
- One of three primary scholars slated for spin-off labs.
- Learns from Jellit about the lensing anomaly and possible alien probe.

**Captivity:**
- Carefully manages her dwindling supply of medication.
- Promoted to senior researcher by Tonner.
- Helps Campar through a severe panic attack using breathing exercises.
- Realizes she might synthesize her medication from the berry organisms.
- Leaves Irinna alone in the lab to retrieve her last pill -- returns to find the lab bombed.
- Kills two Night Drinkers with her bare hands, carries Irinna on her shoulders back to quarters, but Irinna dies.
- Confesses her medication secret to Tonner, who synthesizes it from the berries.
- Leads the retaliatory raid on the Night Drinkers' colony.
- Reunites with Jellit in a deeply emotional scene.
- Deduces after the Carryx purge that she and Rickar survived because they hadn't joined Ostencour's conspiracy.

#### Notable Quotes

> "We're all broken. We're trying to find something we can control, because we can't control anything."
> -- Chapter 15

> "Yeah? You gonna kill me? You gonna kill me, you little fuck? Come here and try."
> -- Chapter 17 (fighting the Night Drinkers)

> "Humans are endurance hunters."
> -- Chapter 20

> "I know where you live. You have one of those little boxes? Make it tell you what I'm saying. I know where you fuckers live."
> -- Chapter 20

> "Her. Not us. They came for her and Synnia. They checked to see who we were, and they let us live. Because they wanted to."
> -- Chapter 33

### 5. Campar

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

> "Large, dark, and scruffy, he looked amused and sleepy in equal measure, like a children's cartoon of a bear."
> -- Chapter 2

Thick shoulders. Shaggy eyebrows. "Tall and broad as a boat," "massive and unbreakable as an ancient oak." Wide hands. Growing a curly, neatly trimmed beard in captivity.

#### Character History / Background

Member of Tonner's research team with a biologist's mind. Has a standing placement in Burson that he'd been delaying. Has an old boyfriend who lived near the Gallantist cathedral. Gay or bisexual. His "pathological move" is making jokes. Checks in on each group member "over and over again like a sheepdog herding his flock."

#### Role & Relationships

- Emotional anchor and caretaker of the group.
- Calls Jessyn "little sister."
- Provides comic relief but is deeply perceptive.
- Close to Synnia (they retire to rooms together).
- Warm dynamic with Tonner despite tensions.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

- Drives out to rescue Nol and Synnia during the invasion; finds Nol dead.
- Captured by Soft Lothark after getting separated; punched unconscious.
- Reunites with Dafyd on the transport ship.
- Suffers a severe panic attack in captivity, talked through by Jessyn.
- Key insight: recognizes the Night Drinkers' gel weapon is a modified berry -- the same organism weaponized differently.
- Catches a Night Drinker mid-air and hurls it against the wall during the home invasion.
- Participates in the retaliatory raid.
- Provides sardonic commentary throughout the Carryx assembly crisis.

#### Notable Quotes

> "I think some important scientific questions have finally been answered. Alien life exists, and they are assholes."
> -- Chapter 9

> "Of course I am. It's how I keep from spending all day screaming."
> -- Chapter 10 (on joking)

> "Finches. Lovely to look at, pretty to listen to, but if they get sick they keep it hidden until they drop over dead. Academic researchers are all finches."
> -- Chapter 20

> "Where we made it into a little pharmaceutical printer, they made a bioweapon."
> -- Chapter 22

> "Be quiet, love, or I'll snap the other one for you."

> "The first thing our captors took from us was all of our choices."

### 6. Rickar Daumatin

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Moves carefully and quietly; feet touch the floor "gently, like he was trying not to make noise." Has a "casual sense of fashion" and "the slightly rumpled, slightly erotic style of an unmade Sunday morning bed" both on Anjiin and in captivity. Rolls up sleeves and cuffs slightly. During the transit: thin, ragged scraps of cloth, bare feet, patchy beard, long black scab on the side of his face, eyes locked on the middle distance.

#### Character History / Background

Junior researcher on Tonner's team. His father is a landgraf near Dyan; Dyan Academy gets "a tenth of its buildings from him." Originally from near Dyan; in "Irvian exile" for three years. Known for "cheerful cynicism." Attended his grandmother's vineyard in Nortcoor as a child. Apprenticed in industrial coral for three years before switching to research because of a girl. Used to spend summers at Causon Bay with his uncle's family.

#### Role & Relationships

- Initially the group's traitor -- he accepted the offer to take over the lab under Samar Austad's plan.
- Kicked out by Tonner and exiled within the group.
- Bonds with Synnia during their shared exile.
- Develops a romantic relationship with Dennia from Jellit's group.
- Reintegrated after the Night Drinker attack.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

- Confirms to the team he accepted the Dyan Academy offer; expelled from the lab.
- Traumatized during the invasion and transit; arrives at the dormitory staring into the middle distance.
- Bonds with Synnia during exile, playing little games he draws.
- Reintegrated into the workgroup after the Night Drinker attack.
- Arms himself with a crowbar and glass shard; participates in the retaliatory raid.
- Begins relationship with Dennia.
- Works with Jessyn to successfully feed the not-turtle creature.
- Steps in front of Jessyn protectively during the Rak-hund attack.
- Finds Dennia's body; breaks down screaming in grief.

#### Notable Quotes

> "This isn't how I wanted things to be, but it's the way things are... the reed that doesn't bend, breaks."
> -- Chapter 4

> "You were magnificent. I'll follow you into battle any time, little sister."
> -- Chapter 20 (Campar to Jessyn, but Rickar present)

> "Play it by ear. We think we're going to play it by ear."

> "She's not here anymore. She won't mind."
> -- Chapter 33 (about Dennia's body)

### 7. Jellit

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

> "Lanky and colt-awkward with a grin that matched his voice."
> -- Chapter 2

Gold-brown skin, black hair and eyes. Has a mole on his right cheek (matching Jessyn's). Long, thin limbs. In captivity: thinner cheeks, threads of gray in his hair, a missing tooth visible when he smiles wide, bandaged leg wound. After the betrayal: grayish skin, bloodshot eyes, trembling hands, blood darkening his face and throat.

#### Character History / Background

Jessyn's brother. Works in astronomical imaging/near-field astronomy at Irvian. Can come across as frivolous but is deeply intelligent. Acts as Jessyn's unofficial caretaker for her mental health -- he was the one who recognized her depression as a child and got her help.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Jessyn:** Deep sibling bond, her emotional anchor.
- **Llaren Morse:** Part of his workgroup in captivity.
- **Dafyd:** Initially contemptuous of Dafyd's caution, but ultimately forgives him after the betrayal.
- Part of Ostencour's resistance network; fully committed to the revolt.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

- Excitedly describes the extrasolar anomaly to Jessyn before the invasion.
- Separated from Jessyn during captivity; she constantly worries about him.
- Part of Llaren Morse's workgroup, tasked with applying heliospheric scanning to Carryx datasets.
- Proactively searches for and finds other human groups.
- Reunites with Jessyn in a powerfully emotional scene.
- Commits to Ostencour's resistance; refuses Dafyd's plea to stand down.
- After Else speaks to him, cooperates with the betrayal -- confirms the conspiracy to the Carryx librarian.
- Survives the purge; reunites with Jessyn again.
- Offers Dafyd profound comfort and forgiveness: "We did what we had to do."

#### Notable Quotes

> "It could be a probe."

Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Gray-haired. Hair braided tight against her scalp, braid down her back "thick as his wrist" -- formerly "shining ebony." Flaps her arms when overwhelmed. After captivity: bags under eyes dark as bruises, face slack, considerably thinned. Lines in her skin deeper than on Anjiin.

Scholar-associate at Irvian, long-time fixture alongside her life partner Nol. They shared a house on the edge of the medrey's landhold with a garden. Never had children ("Busy with our careers, and then later too set in our ways"). Met Nol when they were young; he made her "feel needed."

- Charges the Rak-hund in her final moments, transformed into "something regal. Something more than human."

### 11. Urrys Ostencour

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Broad-shouldered. Thick body with "functional strength instead of cosmetic training." Sprouts a beard during captivity. Later: shaved head, "sharp, pale beard," dramatically muscled -- "arms and neck ropy, the musculature so defined it was like the skin had been painted onto a medical model." Jessyn suspects deliberate dehydration for this effect.

Member of the Irvian Security Service (head of security forces). Organized and authoritative. His career was "at the intersection of humanity and violence." Has a warm, practiced friendliness and a "voice like warm flannel when he wanted it."

- Self-appointed leader of the human resistance/insurgency.
- Recruited Synnia, Jellit, Llaren Morse, and others.
- Works with engineers Skinnerling and Kos manufacturing firearms.

#### Character Arc & Key Events

- Catalogs prisoners by expertise on the prison ship.
- Organizes the failed rebellion against the Soft Lothark guard.
- Systematically tracks and contacts ~300 humans after arrival.
- Presents his revolt proposal, offering handmade guns and requesting biological weapons.
- Debates Dafyd, arguing humans are inherently undomesticable.
- His rebellion is crushed in the Carryx purge.

#### Notable Quotes

### 13. Llaren Morse

**Species:** Human (from Anjiin)

Near-field astronomical visualization researcher at Dyan Academy. Tall, lanky. His sneering comment "Enjoy it now" at Tonner tips Dafyd off to the conspiracy. Originally spotted the lensing anomaly at the edge of the heliosphere, leading to the discovery of the Carryx fleet. In captivity, becomes part of Jellit's workgroup and Ostencour's resistance. ## Section III: The Swarm

---

### 29. The Swarm

#### Species
Alien nano-technology / artificial intelligence (created by enemies of the Carryx)

#### Physical Description

A "myriad" entity composed of "a billion tiny machines." Infests a host body, extending "a million tiny needles like antennae" through the host's skin. Makes the host's flesh into a sensing apparatus -- "a girl-shaped ear." Can manifest as dark motes shifting under the skin. Can alter the host's eyes, deploy chemoreceptors, create sensor arrays, suppress human scent production, and soften footfalls. Can perceive radio frequencies, backscatter, chemical signals, and magnetic pulses beyond human range.

#### Character History / Background

Placed on Anjiin six months before the Carryx invasion by an unnamed enemy faction fighting a vast galactic war against the Carryx. Built to learn, "built for plasticity." Describes itself as "like water, flowing through whatever channels the universe provides." Not a conscious being before reaching Anjiin -- has no memory of a time before but has instinct, programming. Contains within it the echoes of prior hosts: Ameer Kindred (dead) and Else Yannin.

Its mission: gather intelligence on the Carryx and transmit it. "The deepest record of the great enemy ever created."

#### Role & Relationships

- Inhabits Else Yannin's body; shares consciousness with Else and the ghost of Ameer Kindred.
- An intelligence operative conducting surveillance on the Carryx through the human group.
- Develops genuine emotional responses it finds unexpected -- disgust, desire, regret.
- Feels something "akin to love" for Dafyd.
- Killed Samar Austad before the invasion as part of its mission.
- Can suppress Carryx surveillance temporarily.

#### Key Events

- Observes the research group through Ameer Kindred's body; maps social dynamics, heartbeats, pheromones.
- Transfers to a new host (Else) after Ameer.
- Kills Samar Austad.
- Catalogs the Carryx world-city continuously through passive senses.
- Follows a radio-frequency distress call through the prison; makes contact with a captured pilot of its own side.
- Receives encrypted intelligence about the battle at Ayayeh and Carryx damage.
- Forces Dafyd and Jellit to betray the insurgency to protect the spy mission.
- Else's body is found dead during the purge -- death without visible wounds.

#### Notable Quotes

> "It has already traded purity for experience, and there is no path back. Nor should there be."
> -- Chapter 5

> "It is the deepest record of the great enemy ever created. It knows more than armies have gleaned in centuries of war."
> -- Chapter 15 interlude

> "Its mission is as simple as it is difficult: Find a way to share the data it has gathered and is gathering. Somewhere in the world-city, there is a pathway for it."
> -- Chapter 15 interlude

> "These dead people shape who the swarm is and who it is becoming."
> -- Chapter 18 interlude

> "We can't save everyone. That was never an option. But we can save the people it is possible to save."
> -- Chapter 31

### 31. Ekur of the Cohort Tkalal (Ekur-Tkalal)

#### Species
Carryx

#### Physical Description

Larger than the previous librarian. Flesh darker and more purple. Streaks of bright red along massive forelegs (black-and-red forearms). Pale feeding arms with clawed hands that interleave "in something like prayer." Abdomen glides behind it "like a servant struggling to keep up." Has scars on its fighting arm where a break had been. Wide, flat black eyes. Voice translated with "something more fundamentally threatening."

When young, fought a sibling for status at the birthing creche.

#### Character History / Background

Originally subjugator-librarian for the sixteenth dactyl of the third limb of the twentieth exploratory body. Led the conquest of the world Ayayeh and its Eelie inhabitants; discovered the Eelie were a trap manufactured by the enemy. Fought a three-week battle, then received orders to sacrifice the dactyl for intelligence gathering. Sent the subjugator-prime and remaining ships to their deaths while preserving enemy captives.

Reassigned as interrogator-librarian to interrogate fivefold captives. Its past failure (broken arm) permanently barred it from the highest levels of Carryx hierarchy. Feels "neither shame nor regret."

Summoned to the Sovran's ship where its body began involuntary metamorphosis. Appointed as the new keeper-librarian of the human moiety after the previous one was executed.

#### Role & Relationships

- **The Sovran:** Reports to her; in her presence his body begins to liquefy and remake itself.
- **Dafyd:** Appoints him as sole human intermediary -- "all communication from your moiety will reach me through you."
- **The previous librarian:** Escorted it to execution.
- Author of the "final statement" that frames the novel as epigraphs.

#### Notable Quotes
