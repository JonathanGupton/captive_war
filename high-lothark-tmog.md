# High Lothark — Reference in *The Mercy of Gods*

*The Captive's War, Book 1: The Mercy of Gods — James S.A. Corey*

---

## Total Mentions

**One.** A single line in TMoG Chapter 24, with no other reference in either book.

---

## Location and Setting

**Chapter 24** (epub `part0032.xhtml`). Ekur-Tkalal — keeper-librarian of the human moiety, here serving as **interrogator-librarian** — questions a captured deathless pilot. The captives are *"fivefold"* — five-armed/five-limbed beings. They are the same silvery pentapods Campar later finds dead on the disabled command-ship bridge in TFOB Ch 28.

Twenty have survived the Carryx vivisection-and-conditioning intake process. One is held in a dry tank under noxious ultraviolet light. A captured "translation half-mind" (built from previous fivefold captives' chemical-and-electromagnetic communication) carries the conversation between Carryx vocalization and the captive's mist-of-esters / radio-burst speech.

This is also the chapter in which the swarm — wearing Else's body — slips out of the human quarters, climbs through the world-palace, and intercepts the captured deathless's distress call. The interrogation and the swarm's reconnaissance happen in parallel.

## What We Learn About High Lothark

Almost nothing directly. The reference is a one-liner — a bullet-point in Ekur-Tkalal's interior taxonomy of *manufactured life-simulacra* the Carryx have previously catalogued. The category contains three named members:

| Entity | Location |
|--------|----------|
| **Stick-flowers** | Ursin-Qin |
| **Stone-Mind** | **High Lothark** |
| **Ambients** | Cahl and Deáphan |

The line tells us:

- **High Lothark is a place** — a star, system, planet, or region — and not (in this naming) a species.
- It hosts (or hosted) a **Stone-Mind**, which Ekur-Tkalal categorizes as *manufactured life*: a built intelligence rather than an evolved one, distinct from the half-minds the Carryx themselves manufacture.
- The Carryx find such artifacts *"perversions"* and *"depraved"*. The category is not aesthetically neutral to them.
- The Carryx have encountered the Stone-Mind of High Lothark closely enough to have categorized it. Whether they destroyed it, subjugated its makers, or simply documented it is unstated.

## Notable Adjacencies

### "Lothark" is a recurring stem in the Carryx empire's species lexicon.

| Name | Status in books |
|------|-----------------|
| **High Lothark** | Place hosting a manufactured Stone-Mind; mentioned once (TMoG Ch 24). |
| **Soft Lothark** | Active Carryx soldier-jailer client species; squat-bodied, present throughout both books. |
| **Deep Lothark** | A Carryx client species Dafyd consults via slow symbol-conversation in the world-palace gardens; built a culture of resistance "out of skin oil." |

The shared stem strongly suggests these are related — possibly different castes, breeding lines, or substrate variants of one ancestral lineage that the Carryx have, over time, sorted into utility groups. Compare TMoG Part Two's epigraph (Ekur-Tkalal again):

> "The Rak-hund execute our will. If there is any *because* in that, it is because we killed or sterilized all aspects of the Rak-hund that didn't, and we kept the remnant that did."

If the same procedure was applied to the Lothark, *"High Lothark"* might denote a pre-conquest or unsubjugated branch — possibly the original wild form, or possibly a higher-status rebel branch the Carryx eliminated. The fact that the only thing remembered from High Lothark is a *Stone-Mind* — a built intelligence — would fit either a lost civilization that built minds before the Carryx came, or a culled cousin-branch whose technology survived its extermination as a Carryx trophy-category.

This is interpretation. The text says only "Stone-Mind of High Lothark" once.

### Predates Clae's lichen theory by a long way

The interrogation in TMoG Ch 24 establishes — eleven chapters into the first book — that **the Carryx know the deathless are manufactured life**. The captive flatly says so: *"We are designed life... We are like half-minds made from life."* Ekur-Tkalal records this with disgust but also with recognition; it has a category for the phenomenon already.

This is the data point Clae is working from in TFOB Ch 36 when she proposes the lichen theory: the deathless is not a species but an instruction-set. The TMoG interrogation makes Clae's synthesis less of a leap and more of a reading of records the Carryx archive has been keeping all along.

The captive's claim that they were built by the **Aunjeli** — *"flesh is made from semi-stable plasma, and they build their cities in the coronas of stars"* — is then either truth, deflection, or an instance of the same instruction-set wearing yet another substrate. The captive immediately refuses to elaborate. *"Go inseminate your Sovran, we aren't going to tell you feces eaters anything."*

### High Lothark vs. Logothetes of Shun and Sum

Worth noting that *High Lothark* and the *Logothetes of Shun and Sum* (Clae's deepest deathless reference, TFOB Ch 36) are not the same word, despite a passing phonetic similarity. *Lothark* is a recurring Carryx-empire species stem; *Logothetes* is a separate name with separate paired worlds (Shun, Sum). Both sit in the deepest Carryx history adjacent to the deathless or to manufactured-mind phenomena, but the books treat them as distinct entries in the archive.

## Open Questions

- Where is High Lothark? Is it still extant, sterilized, or under Carryx administration?
- Who built the Stone-Mind, and what does it do?
- Is the Stone-Mind related to the deathless instruction-set, to the half-minds the Carryx themselves make, or to something else entirely?
- Is "High Lothark" the homeworld of a "high" branch of the broader Lothark lineage that the Carryx eliminated and replaced with the Soft / Deep variants currently in service?

The book gives the line and moves on. The category it puts the Stone-Mind into — *manufactured life-simulacra* — is, by TFOB Ch 36, the most strategically important category in the empire's archive.
