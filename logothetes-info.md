# The Logothetes — Compiled Information

*The Captive's War — James S.A. Corey*

A complete inventory of every on-page reference to the Logothetes across both books.

---

## Total Mentions

**Two — one in each book.** Both are single-line references, each in a structurally significant place. They almost certainly refer to the same people; together they describe one of the most important pieces of pre-human deep history in the series.

---

## Mention 1 — *The Mercy of Gods*, Part One epigraph

The Logothetes appear in the **Part One ("BEFORE") epigraph** of TMoG, attributed to *"the final statement of Ekur-Tkalal, keeper-librarian of the human moiety of the Carryx."* The epigraph is framed as Ekur-Tkalal speaking in a far-future setting, recounting the empire's history to an interlocutor (the same framing used in the closing scene of TFOB, where the deathless interrogator Carlon questions Garral).

> "You ask how many ages had the Carryx been fighting the long war? That is a meaningless question. The Carryx ruled the stars for epochs. We conquered the Ejia and Kurkst and outdreamt the Eyeless Ones. **We burned the Logothetes until their worlds were windswept glass.** You wish to know of our first encounter with the enemy, but it seems more likely to me that there were many first encounters spread across the face of distance and time in ways that simultaneity cannot map. The ending, though. I saw the beginning of that catastrophe. It was the abasement of an insignificant world that called itself Anjiin."
> — TMoG, Part One epigraph

What this tells us:

- **Method of destruction:** "burned… until their worlds were windswept glass." The same field-weapon technique the Carryx use on Jurupe in TFOB Ch 26 — the *"killing aurora"* that converts a thriving biosphere to red-glowing slag. The Logothetes were sterilized at planetary scale, plural — *worlds*, not a world.
- **Ekur-Tkalal's own taxonomy:** within a single sentence, three different verbs are used for three different fates — *conquered* (Ejia, Kurkst), *outdreamt* (Eyeless Ones), *burned* (Logothetes). The Logothetes received the most absolute treatment — not subjugated, not assimilated as a moiety, not outmanoeuvred. *Erased.*
- **Time scale:** "epochs." The Logothetes' destruction is ancient even in Carryx terms.
- **Adjacency to the long war:** the sentence about burning the Logothetes is positioned immediately before Ekur-Tkalal's pivot to *"your wish to know of our first encounter with the enemy."* The Logothetes are mentioned in the same breath as the deepest history of the Carryx-vs-deathless war.

## Mention 2 — *The Faith of Beasts*, Ch 36 (Clae's lichen theory)

Clae has spent the chapter searching the Carryx archive for references to humans and to the deathless. She brings the result to Dafyd's garden:

> "I went back after we talked to Campar. The archive's too big. I can't be sure. But I found references to what sounds like humans going back eight thousand years. The deathless enemy? The black things that keep fighting even after they're dead? They go back millions. **The earliest references I found, they were associated with something called the Logothetes of Shun and Sum, but even in those it seems like the Carryx had been fighting the deathless before that.**"
> — Clae, TFOB Ch 36

What this tells us:

- **"Of Shun and Sum":** A new qualifier appears in the second mention — *"of Shun and Sum."* Almost certainly the names of two worlds, a binary system, or paired homeworlds (the construction is parallel to other paired Carryx names like *the seven Lek-Variable* or *the Gar of Estian*). This matches the plural *"their worlds were windswept glass"* from TMoG.
- **Time scale, refined:** *"go back millions [of years]"* but the Logothetes are referenced *as one of the earliest known associations* with that millions-of-years-old phenomenon. The Logothetes are old; the deathless are older still. Clae is explicit: *"even in those it seems like the Carryx had been fighting the deathless before that."*
- **Association with the deathless:** the Logothetes appear in the archive alongside the earliest deathless references. The most natural reading is that the Logothetes were a substrate species — an earlier host for the deathless instruction-set. (Clae's lichen theory, delivered in the same conversation, says the deathless is not a species but a design pattern that hops from substrate to substrate; humans and the silvery pentapods are current instances; the Logothetes, in this reading, would be a previous one.)

## Putting the two mentions together

Both mentions are individually slight. Together they sketch one of the series' most important pieces of pre-human history.

### The Logothetes were probably a deathless substrate

Clae's archival work places them at the earliest known intersection of the deathless phenomenon and the Carryx record. Ekur-Tkalal's history places them among the species the Carryx most thoroughly destroyed — "burned… until their worlds were windswept glass," with the same weapon used to deny Jurupe to the deathless in TFOB Ch 26. The pattern is consistent:

> When the Carryx find a world they think the deathless are using, they sterilize it.

The Logothetes' fate matches the fate of Jurupe-scale events, *plural*. They lived on more than one world, and the Carryx burned all of them.

### They are the only named pre-human deathless host

In TFOB, the deathless are seen wearing two substrates: humans (Corvall on World) and the silvery five-tentacled pentapods (the dead crew on the Ashtin-Kah command ship). Both are "current" — alive in the same engagement. The Logothetes are the only name in the books for an *older* host. They are the textual proof that Clae's lichen model has a history: that the substrate has changed before, more than once, and that the Carryx have been killing substrates as fast as they can identify them since the war began.

### They predate the war's known beginning

Clae's caveat is critical: *"even in those it seems like the Carryx had been fighting the deathless before that."* The Logothetes are the **earliest archive reference to the deathless association** but not the **earliest deathless reference**. The implication: the deathless predate any species the Carryx's archive can name, and the Carryx-deathless war is older than the Carryx's own oldest enemy.

### Ekur-Tkalal's "final statement" matters

The TMoG epigraph is delivered by Ekur-Tkalal in a setting where the Carryx empire has already fallen. He speaks to an interlocutor who *"asks how many ages."* The same character who in TFOB's epilogue is implied to be a deathless interrogator (Carlon) is, in this future-frame, almost certainly the listener. Ekur-Tkalal's *first words* about the empire's deep history mention the Logothetes by name. They are the **first thing** Ekur-Tkalal thinks the deathless need to know about the war they have just won. That's an editorial weight the books place on the name.

## What's left unsaid (open questions)

The text does **not** say:

- What the Logothetes looked like, biologically.
- Whether they wore the deathless living-armor or some predecessor form.
- How many worlds they inhabited (only "worlds," plural).
- Where Shun and Sum are, or whether they are still findable as windswept glass.
- Whether the Logothetes were the *original* deathless substrate, an *intermediate* one, or simply *the earliest one the Carryx archive happens to have catalogued*.
- Whether their destruction was effective — i.e., whether burning the worlds actually ended the deathless instruction-set on those substrates, or whether the instruction-set simply migrated to the next substrate the Carryx hadn't yet identified.

The cumulative weight of TFOB's revelations argues against the destruction having been effective. If the deathless is a propagable design pattern rather than a localized species, then sterilizing the Logothetes' worlds would have only forced the pattern to find a new host. The "long war" continues precisely because every Carryx victory at the substrate level is followed by a new substrate appearing somewhere the Carryx weren't watching.

Anjiin is the next name on that list.

---

## Likely Book 3 weight

The Logothetes are a setup. Two single-line references, both in epigraph or revelation positions, both paired with the deepest history of the war, both attached to Ekur-Tkalal or Clae — the two characters in either book most able to think across the archive. The name almost certainly returns in Book 3, in at least one of these registers:

- A specific deathless asset, weapon, or technique whose name traces back to *Shun and Sum*.
- Confirmation that the deathless instruction-set, on losing the Logothete worlds, used the same propagation method that placed Clae inside Ameer/Else/Jellit and the cyst inside Rickar — i.e., that *the swarm and the deathless are the same phenomenon, and the Logothetes were where that phenomenon last had a homeworld.*
- A direct callback in Ekur-Tkalal's "final statement," which the TMoG epigraph quotes only the opening of. The statement clearly continues; the rest is not yet on the page.
