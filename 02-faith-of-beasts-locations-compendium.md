# Locations Compendium -- The Faith of Beasts (James S.A. Corey)

Comprehensive catalog of every planet, world, system, region, and ship/vessel referenced in the text.

---

## Section I: Planets, Worlds, and Star Systems

---

### 1. Anjiin

- **Appearances**: Chapters 1-6 and throughout.
- **Summary**: The human homeworld, primary setting of *The Mercy of Gods*. Home to roughly four and a half billion people before the Carryx invasion; fewer than four thousand were taken to the world-palace. In Anjiin's origin myth (quoted from *Myths of Origin*), a boy named Ke names the world for a bright "twin world" in the night sky "made when the Great Heron split the sky" — mythological, not astronomical, and not identified with the Carryx homeworld, which the text treats as a distant star ("one of the billions"). Anjiin's ecosystem and civilization were shattered by conquest, and characters repeatedly frame it as a home they cannot return to.
- **Key quotes**:
  > "Except in one village at the foot of a great mountain, there was a boy whose name was Ke... the brightest light was not a star, but the twin world that was made when the Great Heron split the sky... he gave it a new name: Anjiin."
  >
  > "Anjiin had been home to four and a half billion people. The Carryx had taken a little under four thousand of them back to serve in the world-palace."

**Regions and institutions on Anjiin referenced in Faith of Beasts:**
- **Scholar's Common** — neighborhood where Ameer Kindred lived in a rental loft.
- **Dyan / Dyan Academy** — city where Rickar planned a comfortable retirement; its academy is a touchstone for comparing Anjiin biology to the planet World.
- **Irvian Research Medrey** — where Jessyn Kaul and Rickar previously worked.
- **Hibbrin Medrey** — archaeology department headed by Garral Pär.
- **Hausen Educational Medrey** — site of Rickar's planned senior position.
- **Itzibahn Common** — southern-hemisphere nation tied to Llian Andermus's operational-security background.
- **Maryangul** — site of a past military conflict in Rickar's grandfather's era.
- **Abbasat**, **Lanasq Republic**, **Astincol**, **Bar**, **Amreth** — locales tied to universities, arts commissions, and the Gallatian church network.

---

### 2. The Carryx Homeworld / World-Palace

- **Appearances**: Chapters 1, 2, 5, 6, 7 and throughout.
- **Summary**: Seat of the Carryx empire and the prison-capital where captured Anjiin humans are housed. Dominated by immense ziggurats that rise from the surface past the atmosphere, with arcs curving up "beyond the atmosphere" and lit by countless windows. Thousands of alien species are segregated into moieties within its labs, gardens, and quarters. Multiple moons are visible from it. The text treats the world-palace as both place and apparatus — part planet, part administrative megastructure.
- **Key quotes**:
  > "To the east, two huge arcs rose up from the planet's surface below, curving up beyond the atmosphere... the dark forms of other ziggurats rose out of the clouds and stretched off to the distant horizon."
  >
  > "The moon on the world-palace... I looked at the sky there a lot, but I never thought about the moon."

---

### 3. World (the Jurupe-system planet)

- **Appearances**: Chapters 3, 9-20.
- **Summary**: A planet recently taken by the Carryx from the "deathless enemy" (Designed Life) without sterilization — a rare prize. Hosts ruined alien cities, orchard-like agricultural structures, burned-out groves, limestone cliffs, extensive cave systems, and several moons. Carryx subject species (including the human research teams) are dispatched to catalog its biology and decipher enemy technology. The shocking discovery there is that the enemy's dominant species is biologically human. Late in the book the Carryx bombard World into near-lifelessness to lure the enemy fleet into the Jurupe engagement.
- **Key quotes**:
  > "The planet you are presently on is named World. It was, until recently, under the control of our enemy. The strategic and tactical victories of the fifth military body were such that the planet was taken without permitting them to sterilize it."
  >
  > "The result bars listed DNA, chloroplasts, mitochondria, cellulose... The species from the city had been human height... Humans were the enemy."
  >
  > "Billions, however, had died, and not only the advanced animals. The ecosystem of the major planet had been unmade."

---

### 4. Jurupe System

- **Appearances**: Chapters 13-20, 22-29.
- **Summary**: Star system containing World and associated moons and outposts, and site of the novel's central military engagement. The Carryx stage their forces here deliberately to draw an overwhelming enemy response, then bombard the planet and trigger a pitched fleet battle. By book's end, the enemy is denied one of its newest holdings but at catastrophic ecological and civilian cost.
- **Key quote**:
  > "For the subjugator-librarian of the third dactyl of the seventh limb of the three hundred and fifty-second exploratory body, this victory was almost incidental. The deathless enemy was denied one of its newest holdings."

---

### 5. Ashtin-Kah System

- **Appearances**: Chapters 13-14.
- **Summary**: Prior Carryx battle system, named only in comparison with Jurupe. Used as a tactical reference point — operations mimicking Ashtin-Kah patterns are judged least likely to succeed in the current campaign. No physical description.
- **Key quote**:
  > "Those which followed tactics similar to the engagement in Ashtin-Kah system -- as successful as they had been there -- were broadly the least likely to succeed in the present fleet configurations."

---

### 6. Estian Morok Starweb

- **Appearances**: Chapter 6.
- **Summary**: Distant posting where a human named Ames Sofar is sent to be examined by local "Gar" for suitability for inclusion in the "arantine." Named-only; no physical description beyond the fact of its remoteness within the Carryx empire.

---

### 7. Other Named Imperial Systems (mentioned only in briefings to the Sovran)

The librarian's reports to the Sovran name several additional systems and territories as imperial-strategic data points. None is physically described or visited in this book, but they establish the scope of the Carryx empire and the active war with the deathless enemy:

- **Caarlast** — system where a Void Dragon tender was inhabited by larvae but survived, and now claims insight into a new technique for deploying void tendrils.
- **Horol-Sa** — planet(s) the deathless enemy was "forced from," containing extensive manufacturing structures whose loss the strategic half-minds believe will weaken enemy fleets in the coreward systems.
- **Dakku** — planet(s) the deathless enemy was "forced from," paired with Horol-Sa in the same strategic brief.
- **Sado** and **Herusant** — systems where "animal unrest" was "burned out" and the animal moieties selected new species to replace the lost populations.
- **"The three hundred worlds of the spinward holdings"** — collectively named imperial territories, described as "quiescent."
- **"The coreward systems"** — generic designation for the enemy fleet's operating region.

> "A tender of the Void Dragons in Caarlast had been inhabited by larvae but not died... The planets that the deathless enemy had been forced from in Horol-Sa and Dakku had extensive manufacturing structures..."

## Section II: Ships, Vessels, and Spacecraft

---

### 1. The World-Palace (ambiguous — structure/vessel)

- **Appearances**: See Section I, entry 2.
- **Summary**: Flagged as ambiguous: the world-palace is repeatedly referenced as a seat of power and city-like enclosure, but its "arcs" rising past the atmosphere and its role housing alien moieties give it ship-like qualities. Best understood as a fixed megastructure anchored to the Carryx homeworld, not a mobile vessel. Listed here for completeness.

---

### 2. The Third Dactyl of the Seventh Limb of the 352nd Exploratory Body

- **Type**: Carryx military fleet (a "dactyl" is a fleet-level organizational unit commanded by a subjugator-librarian).
- **Appearances**: Chapters 1, 4, 13-20, 22-29.
- **Summary**: The Carryx dactyl to which Rickar, Campar, and Ghati belong. Engaged in the Jurupe system operation. Functions as a composite organism of warships, transports, research vessels, and automated assets controlled by its subjugator-librarian. Capable of being "split" (reorganized) under combat pressure. Carries human and alien subjects as research specimens and expendable field assets.
- **Key quotes**:
  > "I have determined that we are in the third dactyl of the seventh limb of the three hundred and fifty-second exploratory body."
  >
  > "The dactyl split again as the subjugator-librarian fashioned its report and ordered a Saren-hund prepared."

---

### 3. Rickar's Warship (Carryx capital-class vessel)

- **Type**: Carryx warship within the third dactyl.
- **Appearances**: Chapters 4, 22-29.
- **Summary**: Massive Carryx military vessel serving as Rickar Daumatin's primary location in the back half of the book. Contains animal quarters (cells), a common room with displays, food dispensers, and species-segregated berths reminiscent of both the world-palace labs and the post-Anjiin prisoners' hold. Subject to combat maneuvering during the Jurupe engagement.
- **Key quote**:
  > "The warship on which he served was immense, but his place in it -- the place many of the animal servants of the Carryx shared -- was restricted."

---

### 4. Jessyn's Exploration / Research Transport

- **Type**: Carryx transport and mobile research base.
- **Appearances**: Chapters 3, 9, 10.
- **Summary**: Carryx vessel that ferries Jessyn Kaul's research cohort from the world-palace to World and serves as their mobile base during early fieldwork. Features bunks, displays, and translator equipment. Pulses and shudders through "asymmetric space" transitions; smooths out in normal space.
- **Key quote**:
  > "She'd gotten used to the low, pulsing hum of the Carryx ship during the time they'd spent in asymmetric space, and then the smooth whine when they'd returned to the universe with physics she knew and understood."

---

### 5. Carryx Landing Craft on World

- **Type**: Mid-sized Carryx landers (generic; not individually named).
- **Appearances**: Chapters 3, 9, 14-15.
- **Summary**: Five Carryx landing ships arranged in a rough semicircle on the surface of World, forming the research base — four already grounded when Jessyn's party arrives and a fifth dropping in shortly after ("as a fifth ship dropped through the air toward them... it only buzzed like a thousand angry beehives, settled on the earth, and went dark"). Referred to later simply as "the five ships that made up their little group." About "a dozen or so stories high," bronze and black, with ramps to the bare soil — much smaller than the city-sized ships that conquered Anjiin. Serve as habitats and laboratories for the subject species deployed to World.
- **Key quote**:
  > "Four of the Carryx ships were on the ground in a rough semicircle. They weren't the city-vast ones that had come to conquer Anjiin. Each of them stood only a dozen or so stories high, bronze and black, with ramps that stretched down to the bare pale soil."

---

### 6. The Captured Enemy Command Ship

- **Type**: Designed Life command vessel (the "deathless enemy").
- **Appearances**: Chapters 22-23.
- **Summary**: The first enemy command-class ship ever disabled without self-destructing. Carryx forces and their subject species attempt to board and strip it for intelligence — a landmark opportunity, since enemy command ships normally immolate themselves rather than be captured. Heavily defended on the interior; contains at least one armored enemy soldier (Corvall). The boarding is cut short by fleet-scale alarms and evacuation orders as the larger Jurupe battle closes in.
- **Key quotes**:
  > "The enemy command ships refuse to be captured... This is the first time that a ship of this configuration has been disabled without ending itself to deny the Carryx the chance to examine it."
  >
  > "All animals will return to the expedition ship at once for evacuation."

---

### 7. Evacuation Pods

- **Type**: Individual biomechanical escape capsules.
- **Appearances**: Chapters 25, 29.
- **Summary**: Silver, organic-looking single-occupant pods deployed to carry subject-species personnel out of the captured command ship during evacuation. Administer a metabolism-slowing treatment so occupants can survive extended transit. At least twelve are deployed. Rickar Daumatin's pod becomes the vehicle for the swarm's sabotage payload — the pod's broadcast discharge vaporizes Rickar's body as it transmits the swarm's findings to its creators.
- **Key quotes**:
  > "This is your evacuation pod. When you take your place in it, you will be given a treatment that we expect to slow your metabolism. You will not resist it."
  >
  > "The energy used for the broadcast unmade the complex molecules that had been Rickar Daumatin's flesh like he was a human bonfire."

---

### 8. Saren-Hund (contingency vessel)

- **Type**: Carryx automated backup / data-preservation craft.
- **Appearances**: Chapter 20.
- **Summary**: Automated Carryx vessel prepared as a fail-safe during the Jurupe engagement. If the dactyl is destroyed, a Saren-hund flees into asymmetric space carrying mission-critical resources so they can be recovered by the rest of the limb — and, failing that, destroyed to keep them from the deathless enemy.
- **Key quote**:
  > "In the event that the dactyl was destroyed, it would send as many of its resources as possible into asymmetric space so that which could not be recovered by the rest of the limb would be destroyed and kept from the deathless enemy."

---

### 9. The Enemy Fleet at Jurupe (Designed Life warships)

- **Type**: Multiple enemy military vessels, some of novel configuration.
- **Appearances**: Chapters 20-23.
- **Summary**: A major enemy fleet drawn into Jurupe system to defend — or avenge — the human population of World. Arrives from multiple insertion vectors in overwhelming force, including several previously unseen ship designs mixed with familiar configurations. Engages the Carryx dactyl in a pitched battle but arrives too late to halt the bombardment of the planet.
- **Key quote**:
  > "The image shifted to a schematic of the whole system: the Carryx fleet, the planet they were bombarding, the impotent enemy rushing in from all directions and too late."

---

### 10. Carryx Weapons Systems (ship-embedded, for completeness)

- **Void Dragons** — entities woken aboard Carryx warships that reshape space and narrow the degrees of freedom available to enemy maneuver. Weapon systems rather than vessels, but integrated into ships.
- **Hardened missiles / field projectors** — generic armaments referenced during the Jurupe battle.

---

## Notes on Ambiguous Entities

- **Dactyl**: Both an organizational unit and a physical fleet. Listed as a ship-entity because the text treats the dactyl as capable of acting, splitting, reporting, and being destroyed.
- **World-Palace**: Cataloged under both planets and ships because its arcs-through-atmosphere description reads partly as megastructure, partly as anchored vessel. Best read as a fixed structure on the Carryx homeworld.
- **Half-minds**: Wearable translators referred to as semi-sentient; not vessels, noted here only to avoid confusion.
- **"Asymmetric space"**: A travel medium, not a location or ship; Carryx ships transit through it to cross interstellar distances, entering/exiting near the heliopause.
