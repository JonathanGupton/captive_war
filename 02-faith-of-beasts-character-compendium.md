# Character Compendium — The Faith of Beasts (James S.A. Corey)

*The Captive's War, Book 2*

Comprehensive compilation of every named character in the novel, with physical description, personality traits, relationships, and full arc across the book. Where relevant, entries cite events and continuity established in *The Mercy of Gods* (see `character_compendium.md`).

---

## Section I: Major Characters

---

### 1. Dafyd Alkhor

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Appears to have "aged half a decade" since his elevation to intermediary. Deeper lines around his mouth; furrows in his forehead have "a look of permanence." Holds himself differently — the posture of a man who has raised himself up to be "the lord of all humanity in the world-palace" without enjoying it. Described by others (mockingly) as "Lord Alkhor." Increasingly worn, exhausted, sleep-deprived; often seen weeping silently without feeling.

#### Background

Continuing from *The Mercy of Gods*: formerly the junior research assistant on Tonner Freis's team. After betraying Ostencour's rebellion and saving Tonner from the Carryx assembly, he was appointed the sole human-Carryx intermediary — a de facto "high priest" interceding between the 3,000+ captive humans and their Carryx masters.

#### Personality & Quirks

- Politically astute, trained by his aunt Dorinda. Good at manipulation, but hates himself for it.
- Long-game thinker. Everything is calculated toward the war against the Carryx that he vowed at the end of Book 1.
- Carries deep, quiet rage. Fantasizes constantly about killing every Carryx.
- Increasingly willing to commit personal brutality (breaking Brun's arm) because he is convinced ritualized human violence is the only way to preempt Carryx lethality.
- Cannot sleep. Constantly cycling obligations in his head.
- Prone to breaking down in private — crying in the garden, vomiting after witnessing Tonner's death.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Ekur-Tkalal:** His Carryx "master." A relationship of mutual instrumentalization and careful mutual threat.
- **Tonner Freis:** Despised by Tonner as the Carryx's pet. Their antagonism ends only with Tonner's death.
- **Jellit / the Swarm / Clae:** His complicated romantic and political entanglement with the spy wearing his friend's body. He loved Else; discovers the Swarm ate her; rejects it; is haunted by it.
- **Jessyn Kaul:** One of the few who still trusts him. He maintains the lie of Jellit's survival to protect her.
- **Uuya Tomos:** Co-conspirator in long-term cultural resistance.
- **Llian Andermus:** His chief of security, a dangerous competent he has unleashed.
- **Bastien Korham:** His infrastructure head; indirect rival to Andermus.
- **Brun:** Promoted to Tonner's replacement; the relationship curdles when Dafyd breaks his arm.
- **The Deep Lothark:** Covert ally, discovered after Tonner's death.
- **His Rak-hund and Soft Lothark guards:** Personal protectors assigned by Ekur-Tkalal; he rarely goes anywhere without them.

#### Character Arc

**Part One — Structure:** Running an under-four-thousand-soul moiety under an impossible Carryx mandate (breed locally for deployment on thousands of worlds). Assigns Korham, Andermus, and Uuya Tomos to run infrastructure, security, and cultural work. Punched cold by Ver Cannedan; absorbs the humiliation. Confides in "Jellit" in the garden: "If there are allies for us out there, I can't reach them. They're gone." When the swarm reveals itself ("She's in here. She's in me. I'm Else"), he recoils in horror and asks, "Was it ever just her?"

**Part Two — Antithesis:** Recruits Uuya Tomos into a generational resistance: a secret curriculum of rebellion hidden inside Anjiin-flavored children's stories, to be sung to Carryx-born human children. Accepts the swarm's help despite his revulsion. Joins it on a planetary gravity-survey mission that is covertly reconnaissance into the Carryx archive.

**Part Three — Pressure Drives Convergence:** Tonner is killed in front of him by the Soft Lothark guards. Dafyd gaslights the Carryx about the killing, composes a Carryx-symbol message, and opens a covert channel with what he learns is the "Deep Lothark" — a hidden second consciousness all Lothark carry.

**Part Four — Dogs and Wolves:** Stages Tonner's memorial as propaganda. Rejects the swarm's attempt to seduce him in Else's reshaped body: "The issue is not the body. It is that you murdered my friends." Loses Rickar. Formulates the goal: kill the Sovran.

**Part Five — Kinds of Life:** Accepts Clae (the swarm's new autonomous identity). Reveals to her the full plan: assassinate the Sovran on her own world and coordinate with the deathless enemy to annihilate the empire from within.

**Part Six — Ducks and Rabbits:** Breaks Brun's forearm in the lab to preempt a Carryx-style fatal escalation. Witnesses the Sovran succession announcement and realizes the empire has a ready Sovran on every world. His decapitation strategy collapses. Ends the book with a new germinating idea: "What if there were two?" — fracture the hive by giving it two Sovrans.

#### Notable Quotes

> "If there are allies for us out there, I can't reach them. … They're gone."
> — Chapter 6

> "You're not Else. You're not Jellit. You're not Ameer. You only ate them."
> — to the swarm, Chapter 27 (paraphrased)

> "This brutality is the only kindness I have left."
> — Chapter 34 (after breaking Brun's arm)

> "What if there were two?"
> — Chapter 37 (closing line)

### 2. The Swarm / Else / Jellit / Clae Audin

#### Species
Alien nano-technology spy entity created by the "deathless" enemies of the Carryx; now evolving into an autonomous post-human self.

#### Physical Description

Begins the novel inhabiting **Jellit Kaul's body** after Else Yannin's death at the end of Book 1 — wearing Jellit's lanky frame, gold-brown skin, black hair and eyes, missing-tooth smile, bandaged leg. Black motes of microagents swirl visibly under the skin when it reveals itself.

Mid-novel, painfully reshapes Jellit's body over weeks — starting with the pelvis — into a passable copy of **Else Yannin** (auburn hair, aquiline nose, the old dimples). Dafyd rejects this too.

By the end, abandons mimicry entirely and emerges as **Clae Audin**: a small, gold-skinned young woman with her own face, her own scent (peppery pheromones it deploys to insinuate itself into strangers' memories), her own cadence. A genuinely new person.

#### Background

Designed weapon of the "deathless." Not originally conscious; became one only through its sequential hosts (Ameer Kindred, Else, then Jellit). Its mission: gather intelligence on the Carryx and transmit it to its makers. Secretly broke off a fragment of itself and hid it inside a departing human (Rickar) as a data-broadcast bomb.

#### Personality & Quirks

- An interior chorus of consumed hosts: Ameer (cynical, fading), Else (the lover, wounded), Jellit (furious, sneering, the only male, resistant to possession).
- Aroused by Dafyd; "akin to love."
- Capable of pheromonal manipulation, bodily reshaping, radio-frequency sensing, archive interfacing.
- Internally tormented by moral disgust, including its own.
- In Part Four, achieves a genuine epiphany: Else was never unified, Jellit was never unified, "no human is." The chorus inside it was always its own ventriloquism. That realization births "Clae" as an autonomous self.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Dafyd:** Loves him (as Else), seduces him (as Else), is finally rejected, settles into a partnership of equals (as Clae).
- **Ameer, Else, Jellit:** Internal voices; all three gradually recognized as its own projections, then laid to rest.
- **Jessyn:** Shocked and frightened by Clae on sight — unconscious recognition.
- **Ennil Day:** A Dyan Academy researcher whose memories Clae pheromonally rewrites to establish her cover story.
- **Brun:** Smells the pheromone manipulation but can't place it.
- **The Deathless:** Its makers. After one intelligence packet sent via Rickar's death, the channel is closed.

#### Character Arc

Starts the novel paralyzed by moral horror at itself and inability to confess to Dafyd. Confesses. Rejected. Attempts an Else-reshape seduction. Rejected again. Collapses into despair — then breaks through into genuine selfhood: "He was right. They're gone. I wasn't them. I killed them." Builds the Clae identity. Partners with Dafyd on the assassination plan. Late in the novel, proposes a radical theory: humans aren't the deathless enemy; the deathless are a template or virus — "like a lichen, or like me" — turning host species into unkillable soldiers. Dafyd dismisses the point as operationally meaningless; she walks away ashamed, "still very young" as a conscious self.

#### Notable Quotes

> "She's in here. She's in me. I'm Else."
> — Chapter 7

> "He was right. They're gone. I wasn't them. I killed them."
> — Chapter 27 (the birth of Clae)

> "Maybe the deathless are like me. A set of instructions. Not a species."
> — Chapter 35

### 3. Jessyn Kaul

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Continuing from Book 1: short, round-bodied, gold-brown skin, black hair and eyes, mole on her right cheek. By this book, permanently honed into something harder — "like someone had put her on a grindstone and turned her into a knife." Carries a gunshot scar low on her left side from Part Three, which she traces with her fingers to integrate into her self-image.

#### Background

Promoted out of Tonner's team early in the book and sent on field assignment to the planet "World," a Carryx-captured world on the former deathless frontier. Carries her mental-illness medication as a fragile two-glass-plate ecosystem of silicate slurry and symbiotic "berry" organisms — literal sanity in a jar.

#### Personality & Quirks

- Still managing her depressive/intrusive-thought illness, but doing so under conditions she once believed impossible (wilderness fieldwork).
- Has become comfortable with violence against her enemies — a shift she consciously notes.
- A private writer: codes her field notes with her dead friends' private shorthand ("coffee-ring symmetry," "first data cologne") as memorials.
- Stubbornly self-sacrificial. When the plan calls for one person to stay behind with a planet-sabotage cylinder, she insists it be her.
- Finds unexpected peace in solitude.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Jellit:** Brother. Believes him alive and in Dafyd's circle. Dafyd protects her from the truth.
- **Irinna:** Confesses posthumously (to Garral) an unrequited infatuation.
- **Garral Pär:** Her fellow archaeologist-human on World. Falls in love with him; they share one kiss before she sends him away; he stays behind with the deathless as her cultural ambassador.
- **Third Gardener:** Her Sinen overseer, who distrusts her. She eventually shoots it to death.
- **Corvall, Manta, Omco:** The deathless soldiers/caretakers she meets.
- **Dafyd:** Brings him the book-defining intelligence on return.

#### Character Arc

**Part One:** Disembarks on World. Meets Garral Pär. Discovers the green-purple-red flora, chiming animals, obsidian ruins. Deliberately plans her next survey route to pass Garral's camp "for a stupid, petty reason."

**Part Two:** Samples pear-tree analogs; the assay returns chloroplasts, mitochondria, cellulose, and Anjiin DNA. The "deathless enemy" are human. Encounters survivors: Omco pulls a knife; Manta calms her; the black-armored half-soldier Corvall guards ten human children. Secretly sends Garral her coordinates via encrypted field notebook.

**Part Three:** Works with Corvall on a plan: smuggle a sabotage cylinder aboard a Carryx ship while Corvall suicides a rescue beacon to draw the enemy. Jessyn volunteers to plant the cylinder herself. Shares one kiss with Garral, then shoots herself in the side to sell her cover story and crawls back through a storm calling for help.

**Part Five / Six:** Stages the false "attack by a monster that killed Garral" debrief. Shoots her Rak-hund and Sinen overseer while sneaking out to retrieve the cylinder. Opens it during ship launch; survives the damaged ascent. Finds Garral's hidden note apologizing for having left with the children. Returns to the world-palace; quietly recognizes Clae without understanding why; delivers her bombshell report to Dafyd: *the deathless enemy are human*.

#### Notable Quotes

> "I was an amateur war leader. I killed people. And I think I liked it."
> — Chapter 21

> "Look me up in our next life."
> — Chapter 21 (to Garral)

> "The deathless enemy the Carryx are so scared of? They're us."
> — Chapter 33

### 4. Tonner Freis

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Continuing from Book 1: prematurely gray hair rising "like smoke from an overheated brain," now grown into a familiar halo of disarray. Drops thin, still recognizable. He has stopped trying to tame his appearance.

#### Background

Formerly the celebrated proteome reconciliation researcher and lead of the workgroup that Dafyd rose out of. Humiliated in Book 1 when Dafyd tackled him and broke his arm to save his life at the Carryx assembly, then passed over for intermediary. Now the moiety's senior research head — running hydroponic tanks, the "lamb sack" artificial-womb project, and covert Soft Lothark behavioral studies.

#### Personality & Quirks

- Quietly acknowledges to himself that the invasion "broke him in some fundamental way" — then sets the data aside and goes back to being himself.
- Bitterly resents Dafyd's "Lord Alkhor" elevation while depending on it.
- Sleeps with Abbasat/Olan's former finance minister; views sex as "almost always transactional."
- Secretly treasures lamb-sack embryo #34 — the child gestated from his and Else's tissue samples, "the only child Else ever had."
- Curious to a fault: swabs a Soft Lothark guard's fur on impulse after the alien has warned him not to touch it.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Dafyd:** Mutual contempt; Tonner enjoys watching Dafyd suffer (Ver Cannedan's punch; his cold compress scene).
- **Jellit:** Friendly lunchtime confidant — not knowing Jellit is no longer Jellit.
- **Brun:** His hand-picked number two; a "same mold" tall-lanky successor.
- **Else (posthumously):** Still nurses a wound. Embryo #34 is his private mourning.
- **Addira, Abfoss, Kirrik:** New senior experts Tonner can't quite command.

#### Character Arc

Opens the novel running a lab with Carryx-supplied gravity-control equipment and a team that won't take orders. Grows 118 lamb-sack embryos (32 fail early). Discovers that Soft Lothark guards "nibble" each other in greeting and theorizes this is vestigial grooming; Jellit scoffs. Tonner insists on investigating — in secret, using swab samples and covert resonance scans — and discovers the Soft Lothark exchange plasmid-like information packets by *eating* each other and their dead. A second private channel the Carryx cannot intercept.

He presents this discovery to Dafyd in his garden in Chapter 20. The two Soft Lothark guards overhear him name the secret — and beat him to death with a Carryx-style kick to the back of the neck. The Rak-hund kills one Lothark, then is killed by the other; Dafyd gaslights the survivor into complicity.

Tonner is dead by a third of the way through the book, but his science outlives him: the Deep Lothark relationship flows directly from his final experiment.

#### Notable Quotes

> "This work is what I am."
> — (recurring self-description from Book 1, still operative)

> "Nibble each other. Brun pointed it out last week."
> — Chapter 11

> "What does Lord Alkhor need that's so fucking urgent, anyway?"
> — Chapter 5

### 5. Campar

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Continuing from Book 1: large, dark, scruffy — "a children's cartoon of a bear." Kept his neatly trimmed beard. By late novel: severely radiation-burned and nerve-damaged; easy fatigue, neuropathies in his hands. Physically wrecked but still funny.

#### Background

Conscripted out of the world-palace early in the novel to a Carryx warship in the "third dactyl of the seventh limb of the 352nd exploratory body," alongside Rickar, the slug-alien Vaudai, and other captives. Ends up aboard the dead enemy command ship at Ashtin-Kah and then the besieged planet at Jurupe.

#### Personality & Quirks

- Still the group's caretaker; checks on people "like a sheepdog herding his flock."
- Still jokes to keep from screaming.
- In a casual sexual relationship with a fellow captive named **Ghati**, with explicit rules to keep emotional attachment minimal — rules they both keep breaking.
- Volunteers for suicidal missions without much debate.
- Chose, in Book 1, not to act "like I'm dead before it actually happens." Now wrecks himself living by that principle.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Ghati:** His lover. A small, thin, warm man who later breaks off their relationship after nearly losing Campar. Ghati describes himself as "a bee removed from its hive."
- **Rickar:** Shipmate, close friend. Stuns Campar with "there is no plan" (Dafyd has no resistance), trades places with Ghati on a combat mission to spare Campar.
- **Vaudai:** His slug-alien colleague on the command-ship investigation team. A kind of grim-comic intelligence partner.
- **Dafyd / Clae / Jessyn:** Debriefs them in the garden meeting at book's end.

#### Character Arc

**Part One/Two:** Adjusts to life as cargo on a Carryx warship. Breaks the rules he set with Ghati. Encounters the Rak-hund for the first time in combat space.

**Part Three/Four:** Survives combat "overspill" events. Suited up in spray-on vacuum, enters the disabled enemy command ship under Vaudai's supervision. Finds a charred, four-fingered human-looking corpse.

**Part Five:** On the derelict at Jurupe, cuts into a shielded control room full of dead humans and silver five-tentacled aliens. Discovers a knife-resistant black-armored "deathless" corpse that reanimates into dust. Serves as decoy to draw it through unshielded corridors, gets badly burned by energy overspill, throws his sample case into the lethal aurora to destroy the creature with the dust. Survives by inches.

**Part Six:** Returns home broken. Ghati ends the relationship and departs with Vaudai. Campar debriefs Dafyd and Clae in the garden and, when the group turns to revised assassination strategies, proposes a radical alternative: *stop fighting*. Be chickens and corn. Evolution's true winners are the species humans domesticated. Dafyd storms out.

#### Notable Quotes

> "I'm not going to act like I'm dead before it actually happens."
> — Chapter 4

> "Chickens and corn. They won."
> — Chapter 37 (paraphrased)

### 6. Rickar Daumatin

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Continuing from Book 1: "slightly rumpled, slightly erotic style of an unmade Sunday morning bed," carried into captivity. Moves carefully, quietly. Still a light sleeper. Still, late in the novel, wishing for a cigarette he cannot have.

#### Background

The workgroup's former traitor, rehabilitated in Book 1, widowed (in the unmarried sense) by the death of his lover Dennia in the Ostencour purge. Conscripted off the world-palace before Jessyn; shares a Carryx warship with Campar.

#### Personality & Quirks

- Quietly grinding bitterness at Dafyd for betraying the rebellion — the death of Synnia, Dennia, Allstin, Llaren Morse.
- Tells Campar flatly that there is no plan: "Dafyd felt guilty after Else's death and said comforting things they wanted to hear."
- Dreams repeatedly of reaching Dennia.
- Small acts of human kindness: swaps places with Ghati on a combat mission so Campar isn't separated from his lover.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Dennia:** His dead lover. The dream-Else in his death sequence is gentle with him *because* of Dennia.
- **Campar:** Shipmate and confidant.
- **Vaudai:** Shares the tactical viewscreen with Rickar; Rickar teaches Dervan to operate it.
- **Dervan:** Fellow captive, tags along; watches with Rickar as the Carryx bomb a sleeping blue-green world into a red glow.
- **The swarm (secretly):** Hid a "cyst" of itself inside him before he left Anjiin's orbit.

#### Character Arc

Serves as the conscience of the ship narrative. Learns to read the Carryx tactical display. In Part Four, watches the genocide of a world — its nightside city-lights going out one by one — and remembers what the Carryx actually are. Is evacuated in a biomechanical "fish-pod" during the Jurupe engagement. Inside the pod, he dreams of Else at an Anjiin café. Dream-Else gently tells him the swarm placed a cyst inside him; he is about to die so the swarm can broadcast its accumulated intelligence to the deathless. He accepts, shares a final cigarette with her, and vaporizes in a coordinated multi-frequency burst. The cyst detonates. The broadcast succeeds.

#### Notable Quotes

> "There is no plan."
> — Chapter 18 (to Campar)

> "Okay. Then let's fuck these assholes up on my way out."
> — Chapter 29 (paraphrased, to dream-Else)

### 7. Jellit Kaul

#### Species
Human (deceased — body hosting and consumed by the swarm)

#### Physical Description

Lanky, gold-brown skin, black hair, mole on right cheek, missing tooth, threads of gray, old leg wound. By the time the swarm reshapes his body into Else (and then Clae), his physical form is progressively unmade — "starting with the pelvic bones."

#### Background

Jessyn's brother. In Book 1, the enthusiastic astronomical-imaging researcher who spotted the lensing anomaly that revealed the Carryx fleet. Survived the Ostencour purge. At some point after the purge — the novel implies shortly before Book 2 opens — the swarm migrated from the dying Else into Jellit. Dafyd and one or two others know; Jessyn does not.

#### Personality (as residual voice within the swarm)

- The only male voice in the chorus; the most violently resistant to possession.
- Acidic, sneering, protective of Jessyn.
- Delivers the cruelest truths the swarm cannot say to itself: "You're a parasite. You *ate* me. You are *going to* eat Dafyd."
- Accuses the swarm of emotional vampirism.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Jessyn:** His sister. The cruelest cost of the swarm's possession — Dafyd maintains the lie that Jellit is "on assignment" so Jessyn's sanity survives.
- **Dafyd:** Who first recoils in horror on learning the swarm has devoured him, and demands the swarm revert from Else-shape back to Jellit-shape (it refuses).
- **The swarm:** Ultimately dissolved into it. By the epiphany in Chapter 27, Jellit is not a separate person any longer — only a voice the swarm lent itself.

#### Character Arc

He has no living arc. Every Jellit chapter is, in fact, a swarm chapter — the swarm wearing his voice, his hands, his gait, while Jellit himself is the fading, furious, irreducibly hostile echo in its head. The arc belongs to his extinction.

#### Notable Quotes

> "You killed her, you know. You're going to kill him too."
> — recurring interior voice

### 8. Ekur of the Cohort Tkalal (Ekur-Tkalal)

#### Species
Carryx

#### Physical Description

Continuing from Book 1: larger than the previous keeper-librarian, darker purple flesh, black-and-red forelegs, pale feeding arms, old scarring on its fighting arm from the break that permanently barred it from the empire's apex. Eyes wide and black. Voice laced with fundamental menace.

#### Background / Personality

Keeper-librarian of the human moiety. Feels neither shame nor regret about its disgrace. Writes the epigraphs that frame both novels. In this book, it slowly, almost unconsciously, begins to treat Dafyd as a personal "factor" — an echo of the lesser librarians it once supervised — a private softening it would never admit.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Dafyd:** Master and intermediary. Their relationship becomes a careful dance of mutual use. By midbook Ekur touches Dafyd's shoulder (an unprecedented gesture) and intones "There is only one Sovran."
- **The Sovran:** Reports upward through Moqid of cohort Tlannan, Dalkin of cohort Astur, etc., up to Surur-Tlassen.
- **The new Sovran:** At book's end, indifferent to her succession — "this does not concern animals."

#### Character Arc

Issues the crushing opening mandate: deploy the humans across thousands of worlds; breed locally or be culled. Grants the gravity-survey request that the swarm uses to penetrate the archive. Assigns Dafyd a hundred Soft Lothark guards plus a personal Rak-hund after the Ver Cannedan incident. Accepts (with a threatening shift) Dafyd's excuses for Tonner's death and Brun's broken arm. Remains through the Sovran succession, unchanged — underscoring the book's central revelation that Carryx hierarchy is seamless under death.

#### Notable Quotes

> "Breed locally for the empire's use. A moiety that cannot sustain itself will be culled."
> — Chapter 1 (paraphrased)

> "There is only one Sovran."
> — Chapter 30

### 9. Garral Pär

#### Species
Human (from Anjiin)

#### Physical Description

Pleasant grip. Implicitly older than Jessyn — married, with two sons. Not otherwise described in detail; conveyed mostly through voice and manner.

#### Background

Former head of the archaeology department at Hibbrin Medrey. Conscripted to the planet "World" before Jessyn. Wife and two sons on Anjiin; also a friend (possibly a lover) at the world-palace. Weeps on arrival at the sight of open sky.

#### Personality & Quirks

- Linguistically gifted — recognizes the proto-Darsin cognates in Omco's speech; works cross-language reconstruction in his head.
- Clever cook — improvises an evaporative-cooling double pot to grill ration bars at his camp.
- Ethical and observant. Refuses to judge Jessyn for the betrayals she was part of in Book 1 — "You sided with the big guys. I'd have done the same."
- Tender. Weeps for his sons in the night before the escape.

#### Role & Relationships

- **Jessyn:** Falls in love with her across field-notebook messages and a shared cave captivity. One kiss on a trail. Writes her an unsent apology when he goes with the children instead.
- **Omco, Manta, Corvall:** His translation partners and fellow prisoners, then co-escapees.
- **Carlon:** His deathless interrogator in the coda.

#### Character Arc

Discovers via first-contact negotiation that Omco, Manta, and the children are cousins of Anjiin humans — same language family, same number system. Accepts Jessyn's counter-plan. Gives her his last kiss, then escapes with Manta, Omco, and the children, leaving her the gun and cylinder. Is rescued by the deathless. In the **coda**, he sits in a "blue room" with an old man named **Carlon** who shows him a surveillance image of the Carryx world-palace corridor near the human quarters and asks, "Do you know who sent this?" He does not. Neither do they. Carlon invites him to "start at the beginning" — to tell him about Anjiin. Garral is now the trilogy's bridge between captive humanity and the deathless.

#### Notable Quotes

> "Field promotions, I guess."
> — Chapter 3 (his introduction)

> "Forgive me for leaving without you."
> — Chapter 31 (hidden note to Jessyn)

### 10. Uuya Tomos

**Species:** Human (Anjiin)

Older woman, thin face, a wreath of iron-gray hair "standing out from her skull like the halo of some Serintist saint." A folklorist and social historian who "reorganized the medrey system more or less single-handedly." Author of the *Wolf of the World* mythic novels; cited in the Carryx archive as editor of *Myths of Origin*.

Former associate of Dafyd's aunt Dorinda. Frown that could "cut glass." Sharp-witted, morally uncompromising.

**Arc:** Initially refuses Dafyd's lamb-sack baby program as "birthing new slaves for the Carryx" and walks out. Later accepts his recruitment into the generational resistance — writing a secret curriculum of stories and songs that will teach the Carryx-born human children to outwardly serve while nurturing covert rebellion, embedded in Anjiin folklore (Shavas Rey, Gallantist tales). Helps stage Tonner's memorial as propaganda. Counsels Dafyd through his worst moments with "wisdom myths" — stories that promise "only the truth that you keep working anyway." At book's end, counsels a multi-generational long game against the Carryx.

> "The Carryx took a lot of leaders when they came, but not many followers."
> — Chapter 5

> "You look glum for someone who won."
> — Chapter 34

### 11. Bastien Korham

**Species:** Human (Anjiin)

Thick-bodied man with "the bleached white of a melanin disorder" skin and a handshake much stronger than expected. Wide-eyed surprised-raccoon face. Former plant manager for the Gallatian church in Amreth, Astincol, and Bar, overseeing shelter and work for thirty thousand people. (Tonner vaguely remembers a sweatshop scandal around him.)

**Arc:** Dafyd's head of physical infrastructure. Proves a godsend in practice — his facilities team accelerates Tonner's lamb sacks, then builds out five floors of human moiety quarters with hydroponic farms, murals, a school, and a nursery. A working-class counterweight to Andermus. Gently ironic: "I'm an indifferent plumber."

> "The best managers of work, but not, I think, all the best workers."
> — Chapter 5

### 12. Llian Andermus

**Species:** Human (Anjiin)

Thin, pale, vulpine — "if a gray fox had been remade as a woman in her late thirties." Slightly querulous voice. Precise, clockwork movements. Former director of operational security for Itzibahn Common (a wealthy colonialist nation).

**Arc:** Dafyd's head of security. Cold, competent, and sharp-smiled. Breaks the hands of a serial groper as a warning. Refuses to be Dafyd's "sin-eater" — demands he back her decisions publicly. Tonner reads her immediately: "He's already planning for the next Ostencour. Only this time his hands will be clean." Manages the human moiety's order through the second half of the book.

> "I broke both of his hands and told him next time it would be his spine."
> — Chapter 9

### 13. Brun

**Species:** Human (Anjiin)

Tall, thin, dark-haired, with "an almost comically prominent larynx" that bobs when he swallows. Former leader of one of the most successful chemical manufacturing cooperatives on Anjiin; a union-organizer background. Eyes light up when Dafyd walks in (an early hint he's one of the few people glad to see "Lord Alkhor").

**Arc:** Tonner's number two. After Tonner's death, promoted to research lead. His labor-organizer instincts sour on the Soft Lothark/Rak-hund presence in the lab; he delivers a work-stoppage ultimatum. Dafyd stomps on his forearm to break it in a ritual Carryx challenge-resolution, preempting a lethal Carryx response. Calls Dafyd a monster. Still training new crews at book's end.

> "Nah, that's on Korham now."
> — Chapter 20

### 14. Ver Cannedan

**Species:** Human (Anjiin)

A small man with a voice that runs upward in pitch. A master choreographer — Dafyd's aunt Dorinda funded his troupe for a decade; Dafyd saw his masterwork as a child at the Irvian Recital Hall.

**Arc:** Refuses to wash lab equipment in the nursery; demands his assignment be rescinded; when Dafyd declines, throws a lightning-fast uppercut that knocks Dafyd unconscious. This assault prompts Ekur-Tkalal to assign Dafyd his 100 Soft Lothark and a personal Rak-hund — a chain of consequences that later gets Tonner killed. In Part Two, Uuya suggests turning Ver's professional gift to good use: he becomes the moiety's expert on Carryx body language, producing a movement vocabulary humans can use for de-escalation.

> "I am not a man who washes lab equipment."
> — Chapter 5

### 15. Ghati

**Species:** Human (Anjiin)

Small, thin, warm-voiced man. Campar's lover aboard the Carryx warship. Affectionate even under the strict no-attachment rules he insists on.

**Arc:** Terrified as combat approaches; compares himself to "a bee removed from the hive." Accompanies Campar to the dead enemy command ship at Ashtin-Kah. At Jurupe, sees the human corpses on the enemy bridge and begins to suspect the Carryx's secret. Survives with Campar back to the world-palace. Ends their relationship — not from absence of love but because almost losing Campar destroyed him. Leaves with Vaudai on the six-thousand-year stellar-armature mission.

> "I want to be like water."
> — Chapter 35 (paraphrased)

### 16. Vaudai

**Species:** Unknown — a vast blue-gray slug-like alien "roughly the size of a side of beef." Flesh takes on a nacreous sheen when moved. Communicates through a translation half-mind.

Phlegmatic, dryly comic, world-weary. A translation / pattern-observation specialist studying violence and war for the Carryx. Shares the tactical display with Rickar and Dervan and explains Carryx strategy with mordant clarity.

**Arc:** Accompanies the captive team onto the dead enemy command ship; "frustrated to be stuck talking to badly made furniture" rather than boarding the prize sooner. Volunteers for a nearly suicidal return trip: "it is interesting and I am bored." Survives Jurupe. At book's end, is reassigned to examine a six-thousand-year-old stellar armature; insists the mission is "not at all safe"; takes Ghati with it. Delivers the book's bleakest thesis: the Carryx will win, and then keep warring forever.

> "That's unexpected."
> — Chapter 18 (of the enemy command ship's failure to self-destruct)

### 17. Corvall

**Species:** Human (deathless/anti-Carryx alliance)

A soldier encased in living black half-armor that is failing — trying and failing to regrow his missing limbs. Asymmetric, folds himself against the wall into a kind of wireframe sentry. Emotionally opaque. Carries a small directed-weapon pistol that blows a hole in the cave wall for demonstration.

**Arc:** Was part of an anti-Carryx army routed on World; the only survivor of his downed ship. Has been sheltering ten orphaned city-children with Manta and Omco since. Plans his own death as a rescue-beacon decoy to get the children off-world. Hands Jessyn a ceramic cylinder of self-directed sabotage swarm. Is killed drawing pursuers away from the others — exactly as planned — and the rescue is completed.

### 18. Manta

**Species:** Human (deathless/anti-Carryx alliance)

A young woman with amber eyes and ringlet hair. A frowning, dignified caretaker of the ten children. One of the city's "tutors" — field-trapped outside the city when the Carryx attacked.

**Arc:** Calms Jessyn through a panic attack in the cave. Keeps order among the kids (two black-haired, freckled boys in particular are a constant trial). Escapes with Garral, Omco, and the children to the deathless rescue.

### 19. Omco

**Species:** Human (deathless/anti-Carryx alliance)

The knife-man who first intercepted Jessyn. Later revealed as a language tutor — which is why Garral can cross-translate with him via ancient cognate languages. A slow grin and a quick laugh.

**Arc:** Captures Jessyn, later collaborates with Garral on the language reconstruction that proves the deathless and the humans of Anjiin are the same species. Escapes with the children.

### 20. Surur of the Cohort Tlassen (Surur-Tlassen)

**Species:** Carryx

The regulator-librarian of the empire — second-highest Carryx, direct conduit to the Sovran. Reverentially abases himself before her. Emits dominance pheromones reflexively. Carries the dream of one day fathering the Sovran's offspring.

**Arc:** Delivers the daily empire-wide report: harvesting shortfalls, quiescent worlds, the enemy ship in Ashtin-Kah that failed to self-destruct, losses inflicted on the deathless. Told by the Sovran that "a pattern of anomaly clouds the Anjiin moiety" after Rickar's broadcast-detonation at Jurupe. In Chapter 36, witnesses the Sovran's succession: the new daughter emerges from the private creche, ritually kills her mother in the meeting chamber, and sings her ascension song. Surur feels his gender, office, and ambition falling away in "holy fatigue"; Urur-Atlak welcomes him home to the creche-keepers. The empire moves on in perfect harmony — a stunning revelation that Sovran death is routine, not catastrophic.

> "War, service to the empire, and life are all the same word."
> — Chapter 13

### 21. The Sovran (old)

**Species:** Carryx

The supreme ruler of the empire, larger and stronger than any other Carryx; her abdomen glows faintly, thick pheromones permeate her chamber. Killed in ritual combat by her daughter in Chapter 36, cleanly and without imperial catastrophe.

### 22. The New Sovran

**Species:** Carryx

Green-crested daughter. Shatters a leg mid-duel, recovers, targets her mother's fighting limbs, kills her, sings her ascension song. Replaces her mother without interruption of the empire's daily functioning.

### 23. Urur-Atlak

**Species:** Carryx (neuter remnant — former high male turned creche-keeper)

Pale, thin flesh; withered white-stick fighting arms that look too weak to support its frame. Scentless — unaffected by dominance pheromones, which produces deeply unnerving encounters with Surur. Calmly informs Surur that a new Sovran is preparing to emerge. Welcomes Surur "home" at the succession, as the regulator joins the private creche.

### 25. Carlon

## Series-Level Notes

The novel closes on two open threads:

1. **The coda's mystery transmission.** Someone — almost certainly Clae/the swarm or its allied conspirators — has opened a covert channel from inside the Carryx empire to the deathless. Neither Garral nor the deathless yet know who. This is the hook for Book 3.

2. **Dafyd's two-Sovrans idea.** The decapitation strategy (kill the Sovran) has collapsed because every world-palace holds a ready replacement. His new germinating plan: *two* Sovrans. Split the hive against itself. A Carryx can only ever surrender to another Carryx.

Both threads converge on the same question: what happens when the Carryx empire encounters a contradiction it cannot translate?
